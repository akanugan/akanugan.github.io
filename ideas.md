## Idea A: "Dark Matter" — Scientific Noir

**Design Movement:** Scientific Brutalism meets Deep Space Aesthetics

**Core Principles:**
1. Darkness as canvas — deep space black with high-contrast neon accents evoke particle collision imagery
2. Data as art — raw numbers, equations, and physics notation used decoratively
3. Precision over decoration — every element earns its place; no ornamental fluff
4. Kinetic energy — motion inspired by particle trajectories

**Color Philosophy:**
- Background: near-black (#0a0a0f) with subtle blue-black gradient
- Primary accent: electric cyan (#00d4ff) — the color of Cherenkov radiation
- Secondary accent: hot magenta (#ff2d78) — collision sparks
- Text: cool white (#e8edf5) and muted slate (#8892a4)
- Emotional intent: conveys the cold precision of physics experiments and the electric excitement of discovery

**Layout Paradigm:**
- Asymmetric split-screen hero: left side has large kinetic typography, right side has animated particle collision graphic
- Sections use full-bleed dark panels with diagonal clip-paths
- Timeline for experience uses a horizontal scroll rail with physics-inspired connectors

**Signature Elements:**
1. Animated particle collision canvas (WebGL/Canvas) in hero background
2. Glowing neon line separators that pulse like detector readouts
3. Monospace code-style labels for section headers (e.g., `[01] EXPERIENCE`)

**Interaction Philosophy:**
- Hover states reveal hidden data (like detector readouts activating)
- Scroll triggers particle burst animations
- Cursor leaves a faint trail of particles

**Animation:**
- Hero: particles spawn from center, scatter outward (simulating LHC collision)
- Section entrance: elements slide in from left with slight blur-to-focus
- Cards: subtle 3D tilt on hover (perspective transform)
- Numbers/stats: count-up animation when entering viewport

**Typography System:**
- Display: "Space Grotesk" (bold, 700) — modern, scientific, slightly geometric
- Body: "IBM Plex Mono" (400) — monospace for that lab-notebook feel
- Accent labels: "Space Grotesk" uppercase with wide letter-spacing

## Idea B: "Quantum State" — Minimalist Scientific Elegance

**Design Movement:** Swiss International Style meets Modern Scientific Publishing

**Core Principles:**
1. Signal over noise — extreme clarity; content breathes in generous whitespace
2. Grid discipline — strict 12-column grid with intentional breaks for emphasis
3. Typographic hierarchy as navigation — size and weight alone guide the eye
4. Restrained color — one accent color used sparingly for maximum impact

**Color Philosophy:**
- Background: warm off-white (#fafaf8) — like quality scientific paper
- Primary accent: deep cobalt (#1a3a6b) — institutional authority
- Secondary: burnt amber (#c4622d) — warmth, human element
- Text: near-black (#1c1c1e) and medium gray (#6b7280)
- Emotional intent: scholarly authority with approachability; the trusted academic

**Layout Paradigm:**
- Single-column with intentional asymmetric indentation
- Large pull-quotes from publications float in wide margins
- Experience section uses a vertical timeline with alternating left/right content blocks
- Skills displayed as a proportional bar chart (data visualization aesthetic)

**Signature Elements:**
1. Thin rule lines (hairlines) used as structural dividers — like journal formatting
2. Large drop-cap initials for section openings
3. Footnote-style citation numbers linking to publications

**Interaction Philosophy:**
- Understated hover effects — subtle underlines, slight color shifts
- Smooth scroll with section indicators in the margin
- No flashy animations; transitions are functional, not decorative

**Animation:**
- Fade-in on scroll (opacity + slight upward translate)
- Horizontal rule draws itself on section entry
- Publication cards expand on hover to show abstract

**Typography System:**
- Display: "Playfair Display" (700) — editorial gravitas
- Body: "Source Serif 4" (400) — readable, academic
- Monospace accents: "JetBrains Mono" for code/technical labels

## Idea C: "Collision Event" — Kinetic Data Visualization Portfolio

**Design Movement:** Constructivism meets Interactive Data Art

**Core Principles:**
1. The portfolio IS the data — visualizations are primary content, not decoration
2. Layered depth — multiple z-layers create spatial richness
3. Motion as meaning — animations communicate the dynamic nature of research
4. Structural geometry — circles, arcs, and radial forms echo detector cross-sections

**Color Philosophy:**
- Background: deep navy (#050d1a) transitioning to dark teal (#0a1f2e)
- Primary: luminous gold (#f5c842) — precision instruments, Nobel Prize aesthetic
- Secondary: electric blue (#4d9fff) — data streams, neural networks
- Tertiary: soft coral (#ff6b6b) — human warmth amid technical content
- Emotional intent: the grandeur of big science with the intimacy of individual discovery

**Layout Paradigm:**
- Radial/circular hero layout — concentric rings like a CMS detector cross-section
- Horizontal sliding panels for each major section (the "sliding graphics" requirement)
- Skills section uses an animated radar/spider chart
- Publications appear as cards on a scrolling ticker tape

**Signature Elements:**
1. CMS detector-inspired circular graphic as hero centerpiece (SVG animated)
2. Horizontal sliding card carousels for experience, projects, and publications
3. Animated connection lines between related skills/technologies

**Interaction Philosophy:**
- Horizontal scroll sections feel like navigating through detector layers
- Hover on experience cards reveals "collision event" particle burst
- Skills radar chart animates on scroll entry

**Animation:**
- Hero: rotating concentric rings (slow, majestic) with particle dots on rings
- Section transitions: horizontal slide with parallax depth
- Cards: lift + glow on hover
- Stats: radial progress bars fill on viewport entry

**Typography System:**
- Display: "Bebas Neue" (400) — bold, industrial, high-impact
- Body: "DM Sans" (400/500) — clean, modern, readable
- Technical: "Fira Code" (400) — code/physics notation
